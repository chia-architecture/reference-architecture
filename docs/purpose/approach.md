# Open contribution model

This architecture is developed as an open and transparent initiative. Cross-border interoperability requires alignment between many stakeholders across countries, disciplines, and organisations. By making [all content publicly available](https://github.com/chia-architecture/reference-architecture) and [open to contribution](../contribute.md), it enables broad participation and continuous improvement.

This architecture impacts a wide range of stakeholders—including clinicians, patients, policymakers, and technical experts—and therefore encourages contributions from all relevant perspectives. Openness supports trust, adoption, and alignment across the region.

---

# Decision-making

At this stage, [decisions](https://github.com/chia-architecture/reference-architecture/pulls) are made by the maintainer, based on input from contributors.

As the initiative grows, governance is expected to evolve towards a more structured and representative governance model, involving regional stakeholders and organisations.

---

# Constraints

The architecture operates within a number of important constraints:

- **Regulatory diversity**: Different legal and policy frameworks across countries  
- **Data sovereignty**: Health data remains under national control  
- **Heterogeneous IT landscapes**: Varying levels of digital maturity and infrastructure  
- **Resource limitations**: Constraints in funding, capacity, and technical expertise  
- **Digital inclusion**: Need to support populations with varying levels of digital access and literacy  
- **Trust and governance**: Requirement for clear agreements on responsibilities, access, and data sharing
- **Diverse populations**: Different languages and cultural backgrounds
