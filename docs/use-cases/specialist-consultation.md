# Specialist Consultation

## Scenario
A healthcare professional in Country A consults a specialist in Country B (remotely or as part of coordinated care) for expertise not available locally.

## Problem today
- Limited access to specialised expertise across borders  
- Clinical information is shared ad hoc (email, phone, informal channels)  
- Lack of standardisation in data exchange  
- Delays in obtaining expert advice  

## Desired situation
- Clinicians can securely share relevant patient data with external specialists  
- Specialist consultation is supported by structured and complete information  
- Advice is integrated into the local care process  
- Collaboration between providers across countries is streamlined  

## Required capabilities
- Secure cross-border data sharing  
- Access to relevant patient summaries and diagnostics  
- Authentication and authorization of healthcare professionals  
- Audit and traceability of access and actions  

## Related building blocks
- Health Data Exchange  
- Patient Summary  
- Provider Registry  
- Identity and Access Management  
- Audit and Logging  

## Impact
- Improved access to specialised expertise  
- Better clinical decision-making  
- Reduced need for patient travel  
- Strengthened regional collaboration and knowledge sharing
