# Use Case: Planned Cross-Border Referral

## Scenario
A patient in Country A is referred to a specialised healthcare provider in Country B for planned treatment (e.g. oncology, cardiology).

## Problem today
- Referral information is incomplete or transferred manually  
- Diagnostic results are not easily shared across borders  
- Repetition of tests due to lack of access to prior data  
- Administrative burden for both patient and providers  

## Desired situation
- Referral information is shared digitally and in advance  
- Receiving provider has access to relevant medical history and diagnostics  
- Care pathway is coordinated across countries  
- Patient experiences a seamless transition between providers  

## Required capabilities
- Cross-border patient identification  
- Structured referral and clinical document exchange  
- Secure sharing of diagnostic results (e.g. lab, imaging)  
- Consent and access control across jurisdictions  

## Related building blocks
- Patient Identity  
- Health Data Exchange  
- Clinical Document Sharing  
- Consent Management  
- Provider Registry  

## Impact
- Reduced duplication of diagnostics  
- Faster and better prepared treatment  
- Improved patient experience  
- More efficient use of specialised healthcare capacity
